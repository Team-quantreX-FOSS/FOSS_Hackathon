📚 Financial Literacy
💰
What is a CIBIL Score?
A 3-digit number (300–900) that tells lenders how creditworthy you are. Above 750 is considered excellent. Pay EMIs on time and avoid multiple loan applications to keep it high.
📈
Rule of 72
Divide 72 by your annual interest rate to find out how many years it takes to double your money. At 8% interest, your investment doubles in 9 years.
🏦
FD vs Mutual Funds
Fixed Deposits offer guaranteed returns (6–7%) with no risk. Mutual Funds offer higher potential returns (10–15%) but with market risk. Choose based on your risk appetite.
🛡
Emergency Fund
Always keep 3–6 months of expenses saved in a liquid account. This protects you from sudden job loss, medical emergencies, or unexpected bills without taking loans.
📊
50-30-20 Rule
Allocate 50% of income to needs (rent, food, bills), 30% to wants (entertainment, shopping), and 20% to savings and investments. A simple framework for healthy finances.
🔄
Compound Interest
Interest earned on both your principal and previously earned interest. Starting early makes a massive difference — ₹10,000 at 10% for 30 years grows to ₹1.75 lakh.
